looked into it some more. summary of results:

The biggest factors influencing who could speak are:

  • Spending money on the steam account (50%, p. <0.001)
  • TF2 casual rank (1.5% per 10 levels, p. < 0.001)
  • having steam guard (4%, p. 0.14)
  • never using a vpn (4%, p. 0.27)

This is fit with a r2 of 0.31 and a standard error of 41%. Meaning it's not a very helpful prediction in practice.

summary of data

Question 1: How does the amount of "trust factors" a player have impact their chance of having the ability to speak?

There is a very strong relationship between having more trust factors (eg linking your phone, purchasing stuff, never using a vpn) and having the ability to speak. of the 266 responders who met all 5 trust factors, 75% had access to speak, and of the 330 who had 4/5 trust factors, 50% had the ability to speak.

Question 2: How does the account's age impact their chance of having the ability to speak?

There was a positive relationship between account age and both meeting trust factors and having the ability to speak. While both age and trust factors were correlated to the ability to speak, age had a correlation with significance p = 0.003 (r2 = 0.66) and trust had a correlation with significance p = 0.00001 (r2 = 0.95) indicating that trust had the stronger correlation.

Question 3: How does hours played impact the of having the ability to speak?

Similar to account age, hours played did not seem to play a significant role, demonstrating a neutral relationship when interpreted cumulatively.

Question 4: Does the amount of alts a player has play a role in ftp chat access?

Interestingly, only when a player had 1-2 alts, and this does not track with "trust factors". I suspect this may be due to players evading VAC bans or similar.

Question 5: What about TF2 level?

Similar pattern to cumulative hours: strong neutral relationship.
